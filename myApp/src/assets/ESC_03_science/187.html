The alignment of the moon and the sun determines
the phases of the moon.
The crescent moon grows till it is about half full in the
phase we call ‘ﬁrst quarter’. From there it grows to full
moon and then begins to shrink back to third quarter
and ﬁnally back to new moon.
Key facts: The curious (but
also explainable) thing is, that
both movements, rotation and
translation, take the same time
to complete a cycle; That’s why
the same face of the moon is
always pointing to the Earth.
