The skeletal system.
This system is used so that we can stand by ourselves.
It holds us up, it involves the bones, cartilages, joints,
and ligaments.
The bones are made of calcium and phosphorus,
they are hard enough to give us support and hold
our body up. The cartilage is used as a supporting
structure and gives some mobility to joints.
Ligaments are strong and they work to make the
bones and muscles stay in they place. Joints are the
unions of two or more bones.
An adult has 206 bones.
A newborn has 215 bones, that’s because the
newborn’s bones begin to stick together and harden
when they grow up.
