Solubility and insolubility.
The substances that are dissolved in water
completely and are not visible are called soluble.
As example is the sugar, when this is mixed with
water, it really gets dissolved,
the water changed
completely but there were no sugar in a simple sight.
The insoluble substances are the ones that can’t be
dissolved in water. For example: the oil. It can be
combined but never mixed.
