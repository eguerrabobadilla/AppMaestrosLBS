Magnets are objects that produce an area of
magnetic force called ‘magnetic ﬁeld’. The force
that pushes objects away, or attracts them is called
‘magnetism’.
Magnets only attract certain types of metals;
, other materials such as glass, plastic and wood
aren’t attracted. Metals such as iron, nickel and cobalt
are attracted to magnets.
Magnets have a magnetic north pole and a magnetic
south pole. If the same pole of two magnets are
placed near each other they will push away (repel),
while if diﬀerent poles are placed near each other
they will pull together (attract).
